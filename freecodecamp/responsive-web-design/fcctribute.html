Buckethead

From chicken coop to main stage

Buckethead playing a sold out show
Buckethead, whose real name is Brian Patrick Carroll, has had a prolific career, releasing 305 studio albums, four special releases, and one EP. He's also performed on more than 50 other albums by other artists since 1988. A more in-depth look at Buckethead can be found on his Wikipedia entry. And if you want to check out his music for purchase, you can find him on Spotify.
1981
Began playing guitar at the age of 12. His early teachers include Max McGuire, Johnny Fortune, Mark Hammond, Pebber Brown and Paul Gilbert.
1988
The Buckethead character is born after watching Halloween 4, buying a mask, and eating KFC for dinner.
1992
Releases his first album Bucketheadland.
1992
Supergroup Praxis is formed, featuring Bill Laswell, Bernie Worrell, Bootsy Collins, and Bryan "Brain" Mantia.
1997
Contribute to movie soundtracks, appearing on Beverly Hills Ninja and Mortal Kombat: Annihilation, the sequel to Mortal Kombat.
2000-2004
Performs as a member of Guns N' Roses, even recording on their Chinese Democracy.
2005
Enter The Chicken is released under the Buckethead & Friends name, featuring some of Buckethead's musician friends.
2013
Releases 31 albums.
2014-2015
178 Pikes - albums released as a concept to resemble a comic book style and be shorter in length than his previous works at around half an hour in length - are released.
2015
Releases 118 albums, totaling 236 albums.
Since leaving his various bands between 2010 and 2013, Buckethead has returned to playing solo live shows. He recently released his first live album, Live From Bucketheadland.
